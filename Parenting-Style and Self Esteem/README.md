# Parenting-Styles-and-Self-Esteem
This research examined how different parenting styles influence adolescents' self-esteem aged 12–19 years in selected secondary schools in Oyo State, Nigeria.   

##  Method
A total of 388 students participated in the study, and data were analyzed using SPSS to determine the correlation between parenting styles and self-esteem. 

##  Key Findings 
Most adolescents (about 99%) reported an authoritative parenting style, characterised by warmth, communication, and consistent guidance. 
Participants demonstrated high self-esteem, with 95%–100% expressing confidence and self-worth. 
A positive correlation existed between authoritative parenting and self-esteem (r = 0.366, p < 0.001). 
Authoritarian (r = -0.470, p < 0.001), permissive (r = -0.145, p = 0.004), and neglectful (r = -0.106, p = 0.038) parenting styles were negatively associated with self-esteem. 
Middle-born adolescents and those living with relatives had the highest levels of self-esteem. 

##  Key Insight 
The study highlights that adolescents raised under supportive, communicative (authoritative) parents develop stronger self-esteem and positive self-image. Promoting such parenting practices can enhance adolescent mental well-being and social adjustment.
