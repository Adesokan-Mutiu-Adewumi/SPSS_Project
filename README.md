# TITLE: Evaluating Market Demand, Customer Satisfaction, and Growth Opportunities for Geriatric Care Services: A Case Study of a Geriatric Centre

## Objectives
1.	Assess the level of awareness of market demand for geriatric care services.
2.	Evaluate customer satisfaction with existing geriatric care services.
3.	Examine challenges affecting geriatric care service delivery.
4.	Identify potential opportunities for growth and expansion within the geriatric care sector.
5.	Identify factors associated with awareness of market demand for geriatric care services.
6.	Identify factors associated with customer satisfaction for geriatric care services.

## Method
As a Data Analyst, the data was sent in an Excel file. The dataset was first moved to SPSS. Coding was done, followed by data cleaning, and then data preparation for data analysis.

### Instrument
The question for measuring awareness, customer satisfaction and potential opportunity was measured on a 5-point Likert scale of Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree.

For awareness and customer satisfaction, participants' responses were scored. The score values' average was generated. This average score was used to group the scores into 2 categories. A score of mean and above was categorised as well aware, and a score below the mean was categorised as not well aware, for awareness. The same method was used to group satisfaction into satisfied and not satisfied, for customer satisfaction.

### Method of Analysis
Frequency table and charts were used to present the results, while Chi-Square was used to find the variable associated with the awareness and customer satisfaction, at 5% significant level. A p-value less than or equal to 0.05 was considered significant.

## Key Insight
There is a positive perception of geriatric care services among the participants. Approximately 70% of respondents demonstrated good awareness of the value and benefits of professional geriatric care, while 64% expressed overall satisfaction with the services received. Participants strongly recognized the role of geriatric services in improving the quality of life of older adults. They acknowledged the increasing demand for elderly care due to growing caregiving challenges within families.

Despite these positive perceptions, affordability and accessibility emerged as major factors influencing the utilisation of geriatric services. Financial constraints were the most frequently reported challenge among caregivers, highlighting the need for more affordable care options and policy support.

The study also identified substantial opportunities for growth within the geriatric care sector. Most respondents supported expanding home-based care services, telemedicine, wellness programmes, awareness campaigns, and partnerships with healthcare institutions. Furthermore, relationship to the elderly person was the only socio-demographic factor significantly associated with awareness of the market value of geriatric services (p = 0.029), while customer satisfaction did not significantly vary across socio-demographic groups.
