#  Title
Baseline Knowledge and Attitudes Toward Sexual Literacy Among Female Adolescents in Selected Secondary Schools in Ibadan Metropolis, Nigeria

##  Objectives
To investigate the baseline knowledge towards sexual literacy, and its factors among female adolescents in selected secondary schools within Ibadan
To investigate the attitude towards sexual literacy and its factors among female adolescents in selected secondary schools within Ibadan

##  Methods
A cross-sectional study was conducted among 180 female adolescents aged 13–19 years in two public secondary schools in Ibadan Metropolis, Oyo State, Nigeria. Data were collected using a structured questionnaire assessing socio-demographic characteristics, family characteristics, knowledge, and attitudes towards sexual literacy.
##  Statistical Tool
**Frequency table, Charts, Chi-Square, Fisher Exact and Logistic Regression**

##  Software
SPSS, Microsoft Excel and Microsoft Word

##  Results
###  Knowledge
Only 10 (5.6%) participants had good knowledge of sexual literacy, while 170 (94.4%) had poor knowledge

Internet access (p=0.003) and mobile phone access (p=0.007) were significantly associated with knowledge but were not independent predictors.

Positive attitude was significantly associated with better knowledge (p=0.042).

### Attitude
Less than half, 30 (16.7%), exhibited positive attitudes, whereas 150 (83.3%) had negative attitudes. 
<img width="752" height="452" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fc80bd4f-8e27-4902-aef3-8dc3c889e2f7" />

Living arrangement (p=0.037), mother’s occupation (p=0.003), family financial status (p=0.005), internet access (p=0.019), and mobile phone access (p=0.010) were significantly associated with attitude. 
 <img width="1322" height="862" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/054c1e92-7bdc-4f40-b43f-74538442734e" />

However, family financial status was the only independent predictor of attitude. No socio-demographic characteristic was significantly associated with knowledge or attitude.
<img width="1328" height="818" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4fea9ba9-6280-426c-83e1-22a73a941c61" />


##  Key Insight
Female adolescents demonstrated poor knowledge and predominantly negative attitudes towards sexual literacy. Positive attitude was associated with better knowledge, while family financial status independently predicted attitude. Interventions are needed to improve sexual literacy among female adolescents
